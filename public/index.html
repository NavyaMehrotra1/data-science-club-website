Frequently Asked Questions

What will I do in the data science club?

+

All members of the data science club will learn data science related skills (relevant datasets, libraries, machine learning methods) and then apply them in projects. We start the semester with Data Science 101 workshops to teach the basics of data science and machine learning. Later in the semester, we work in teams on data science projects based on different themes.

Who can join the Data Science Club?

+

Any Johns Hopkins student with an interest in data science can join! We welcome students from all majors and years.

What kind of projects will I work on?

+

The projects are data science and machine learning projects based on themes such as Data Science in Medicine or Finance. These can be worked on in groups and as a club, we will be working on each theme for one month (members are free to continue their projects later too!). Some project ideas include: medical image classification, predicting disease risk, fraud detection, stock price movement prediction etc.

Do I need to have data science experience?

+

No prior experience required! We go through all essential skills in our data science 101 workshops. We will also host introductory workshops before we start working on themed projects (for example: Data Science in Medicine Kickoff Workshop).

What are themes, workshops and data labs?

+

Themes are broad areas in which we will explore data science applications (for example: data science in medicine, data science in finance etc.).
Workshops are guided introductory sessions to understand tools, technologies and programming languages for data science. Workshops are interactive and include in-class exercises.
Data labs are general body meetings (GBMs) in which club members collaborate on projects.
